The Long-Term Wealth-Building Benefits of Home Ownership

Homeownership has been shown again and again to be a reliable path to financial success for many generations. While it is true that the performance of the past does not guarantee the outcomes of the future and that prices have fallen in certain years, the value of the majority of properties has constantly increased over the course of the long term.

The huge disparity in total family wealth that exists between homeowners and renters is illustrative of the many monetary advantages that are associated with home ownership. When compared to renters, homeowners have an average net worth of close to $300,000, while renters only have a net value of around $8,000 on average. The majority of families who fall into the middle class may attribute a significant portion of the wealth disparity between homeowners and renters to growing housing prices.

When it comes to amassing money, however, homeowners have an advantage not just due to growing real estate prices but also due to other factors. The ability to weather the effects of inflation, accumulate wealth, and benefit from favorable tax treatment are all benefits that homeowners enjoy that renters do not.
